Élong Canton

H.M. Éhodé Mbébi Michel — Superior Chief of Élong Canton

History & Identity

Origins of the Canton

Elevated to a 2nd-degree Superior Chieftaincy by Decree No. 16 of 19 January 1982, Élong Canton belongs to the wider Sawa group, more specifically to the Bakundu sub-group and the Mbo clan. Located in the Mélong district (which takes its name from it), Moungo department, Littoral region, it takes its name from Élong — one of the thirteen sons of Ngo, known as "Elung Ngoeh". Having left the Congo during the great Bantu migrations between the late 16th and early 17th centuries, its ancestors reached the Gulf of Guinea, then, leaving their Duala cousins in the estuary, continued their journey to the foot of Mount Cameroon before scattering under the effect of volcanic eruptions.

The canton, neighbouring Mbo Canton with which it shares a common ancestry, is seated at the village of Mouanguel and comprises nine villages: Nninong, Mboassoum, Ndoko, Ekolbouni, Mouanguel, Elimbo, Nzobi, Moangwekan and Ediengo — as shown on its official coat of arms. Its current Superior Chief, His Majesty Éhodé Mbébi Michel, leads a resilient community of around 3,000 people, historically drawn to hunting and gathering, today devoted to growing robusta coffee on the fertile volcanic soils of Mount Manengouba. Recently admitted into the Ngondo in accordance with the institution's statutes, the Élong people have found here a place of belonging within the great Sawa brotherhood — a region otherwise rich in tourist sites, from Lake Manengouba to the Ekom Falls.
