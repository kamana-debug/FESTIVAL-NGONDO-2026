Peoples of the Coast

The 13 Cantons of the Sawa People

The Sawa people — guardians of the Wouri River for more than three centuries — gather every December to honour their ancestors and celebrate their shared identity under the banner of the Ngondo.

1
Bassa du Wouri Canton
H.M. Mbodi Épée Gaston
Superior Chief · President of the Ngondo 2026

First settlers of the Wouri riverbanks alongside the Bakoko. The largest of the Wouri cantons (75% of the department), spanning 23 villages between the Douala 3 and 5 districts. A people of farmers, hunters and fishermen, the Bassa share a 500-year common history with the Duala.

🏛️ Presidency 2026
2
Bèlè Bèlè Canton
H.M. Paul Milord Mbappe Bwanga
Superior Chief of Bèlè Bèlè Canton

Founded by Bèlè ba Doo, who crossed the Wouri to build a new kingdom. Its territory stretches to the edge of the Moungo department. Historic bearer of the KIEL'A NGONDO vision — the Ngondo of tomorrow.

🔱 Superior Chief
3
Bonambela (Akwa) Canton
H.M. Dr Louis Din Dika Akwa
King Akwa · Superior Chief

Kingdom of the legendary King Dika Mpondo, signatory of the 1884 Germano-Duala treaty. Made up of 20 villages under the emblem of the Eagle. Mbela Mbengue (North) and Mbela Jedu (South). The canton is home to Douala's commercial heart.

🦅 The Royal Eagle
4
Njo Njo (Bell) Canton
H.M. Jean-Yves Eboumbou Douala Manga Bell
King Bell · Superior Chief

Canton of great anti-colonial resistance. Rudolf Duala Manga Bell was hanged on 8 August 1914 for opposing the seizure of Bonanjo lands. The Pagoda, built in 1905, and the Port of Douala both find their historical setting here.

⚔️ Historic Resistance
5
Deïdo Canton
H.M. Ekwalla Essaka Frédéric James
Superior Chief of Deïdo Canton

Canton whose eponymous ancestor Ebele left his home village for the coast in search of salt. Five lineages make up the Deïdo clan: Bonajinge, Bonamoudourou, Bonateki, Bonatene, Bonantone. Renowned for their solidarity — the "Deido Match".

🌊 People of the Water
6
Bonkeng-Penja Canton
H.M. Dissake Dieudonné
Superior Chief · 2nd Vice-President of the Ngondo

Canton with fertile soil thanks to the lava of Mount Cameroon. Birthplace of the famous Penja Pepper, internationally certified. Three communities — Bonkeng, Bafun and Abo — have lived in perfect harmony for centuries.

🌶️ Penja Pepper PGI
7
Bodiman Canton
H.M. Ngalle Edouard
Superior Chief of Bodiman Canton

The Bodiman, descendants of Mbondo II Male known as "Dima", settled in the 18th century along the banks of the Nkam. Their legendary forest of 51 sacred baobabs — Koro Mabuma — in Mandimba is an exceptional historical and cultural heritage site.

🌳 51 Sacred Baobabs
8
Bakoko Wouri Canton
H.M. Songue Madiba Erick Jamil
Superior Chief of Bakoko Wouri Canton

The Bakoko clan, led by Ngahe, son of Pam, arrived on the banks of the Wouri towards the end of the 16th century. Guardians of the Bissima cult (water deities), the canton is today home to the Japoma multi-sport stadium, a flagship of Cameroonian sport.

🏟️ Japoma Stadium
9
Wouri Bwélé Canton
H.M. Edimo Alexandre Martin
Superior Chief of Wouri Bwélé Canton

A 237 km² canton on the edge of Douala 5, bringing together 16 riverside and inland villages. The 2026 Caravan made a stop here on 28 June — a memorable stage marked by sporting fervour and the passing on of memory around the theme of Awakening.

🌊 2026 Caravan
10
Wouri Bosua Canton
H.M. Superior Chief of Wouri Bosua
Superior Chief of Wouri Bosua Canton

A people of the water par excellence, the sons of Wouri Bosua (108 km², 8 villages) are renowned for their ease on the water and are regularly called upon in the pirogue races. Tradition holds that they share a special communion with the Miengu, the water spirits.

🛶 Masters of the River
11
Bakoko-Moungo Canton
H.M. Njoke Essane Njoke
Superior Chief of Bakoko-Moungo Canton

A Bakoko community established even before contact with the Germans in 1885. The history of its chiefdom, marked by the episode of the deposed chief Nkombe Bea and the fearless Mpah Sem, reflects centuries of community justice tradition.

⚖️ Ancestral Justice
12
Malimba Canton
H.M. Me Matanda Eugene Jacques
Superior Chief of Malimba Canton (Edéa)

A Sawa canton through and through — from the mouth of the Sanaga River to the town of Edéa. The four great Malimba families (Bongo, Mulimbe Mbengue, Mulimbe Jedu, Mulongo) share a love of clams — behona in Malimba — an exclusive culinary specialty.

🦪 Behona — Specialty
13
Elong Canton
H.M. Ehodé Mbébi Michel
Superior Chief of Elong Canton

A canton of the Sawa people, rich in history and ancestral traditions passed down from generation to generation along the Wouri. Its people proudly carry on the rites and customs that make up the cultural wealth of the Ngondo.

🌿 Sawa Tradition
